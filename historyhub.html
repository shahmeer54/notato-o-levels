Mughal Empire

Short overview

Mughal Empire (1526‚Äì1857) was founded by Babur after the victory at Panipat. It brought Persianate culture, big courtly patronage for arts, and monumental architecture like the Taj Mahal. Key rulers: Babur, Humayun, Akbar (consolidation & religious policy), Jahangir, Shah Jahan (architecture peak), Aurangzeb (expansion and later decline).

Culture & Achievements

  • Architecture: Taj Mahal, Red Fort, Fatehpur Sikri.
  • Painting: Mughal miniature style blended Persian and Indian themes.
  • Administration: Centralized revenue systems and court ranks (mansabdari).

Legacy

Language, cuisine, art and many buildings still shape South Asian identity. The empire's decline opened the way for British colonial rule.

Indus Valley Civilization

Short overview

(c. 3300‚Äì1300 BCE) Indus Valley Civilization was known for its planned cities like Harappa and Mohenjo-daro. Streets were grid-patterned, houses had baked-brick walls and private wells. They had standardized weights and evidence of long-distance trade with Mesopotamia.

Notable features

  • Urban planning & drainage systems.
  • Seals with undeciphered script ‚Äî so many mysteries remain.
  • Skilled craft production: beads, metallurgy, pottery.

Why it matters

It shows early complex urban life on the subcontinent and connects ancient trade networks across Asia.

Mohenjo-daro

City highlight

Mohenjo-daro (now in Sindh, Pakistan) was one of the largest settlements of the Indus Valley. Its Great Bath suggests ritual activities. The city's careful planning shows advanced civic organization more than 4,000 years ago.

Interesting facts

  • Great Bath ‚Äî possible public / ritual bathing structure.
  • Wide streets and cisterns for water management.
  • Script remains undeciphered ‚Äî identity of people still debated.
